# Flip 7 — Complete Ruleset

**Players:** 3–18 · **Goal:** Be the first to reach **200+ points**, scored across multiple rounds.

Flip 7 is a push-your-luck game: keep flipping cards to build points, but flip a
**duplicate number and you bust** (lose everything that round).

---

## Deck Composition (94 cards)

### Number cards (79 total)

The count of each card equals its face value, with one exception (the `0`):

| Card | Copies | | Card | Copies |
|------|--------|---|------|--------|
| 0    | 1      | | 7    | 7      |
| 1    | 1      | | 8    | 8      |
| 2    | 2      | | 9    | 9      |
| 3    | 3      | | 10   | 10     |
| 4    | 4      | | 11   | 11     |
| 5    | 5      | | 12   | 12     |
| 6    | 6      | |      |        |

(So there's one `0`, one `1`, two `2`s … up to twelve `12`s.)

### Modifier cards (6 total)

One each of: **+2, +4, +6, +8, +10, and ×2**

### Action cards (9 total)

Three each of: **Freeze, Flip Three, and Second Chance**

---

## How a Round Works

1. The dealer deals **one card face-up to each player**, going around the table.
2. On your turn you choose: **"Hit"** (take another card) or **"Stay"** (lock in your
   cards and sit out the rest of the round, banking your points).
3. Play continues around the table until every player has either **stayed, busted, been
   frozen, or hit Flip 7**.
4. Then everyone scores, points are added to running totals, and a new round begins with
   a reshuffled deck.

### Busting

If you flip a **number card that duplicates one you already have**, you **bust**: you're
out of the round and score **0 points** for it (a Second Chance card can save you — see
below). Modifier and action cards can **never** cause a bust.

### The "Flip 7" bonus 🎉

If you collect **7 unique number cards**, you immediately **Flip 7**: the round **ends
instantly for all players** and you score a **+15 bonus** on top of your card total.
(Only *number* cards count toward the 7 — modifiers and action cards don't.)

---

## Modifier Cards

Modifiers are kept in front of you and **never bust you**. They only apply if you stay or
are frozen (not if you bust).

- **+2 / +4 / +6 / +8 / +10** — add that many flat points to your round score.
- **×2** — **doubles the sum of your number cards only** (not the +15 bonus, not the +X
  modifiers).

---

## Action Cards

**When you flip an action card, you must immediately give it to an active player —
yourself or anyone still in the round — and it resolves at once.** (If you're the only
player left active, you must use it on yourself.) Action cards are also "safe" and never
bust.

- **❄️ Freeze** — The targeted player must **stay immediately**: they bank their current
  points and are out for the rest of the round. (A Second Chance does **not** block a
  Freeze.)

- **🔄 Flip Three** — The targeted player must **flip 3 cards, one at a time**. The
  sequence ends early if they **bust** or **hit Flip 7**. Any **action cards** revealed
  during a Flip Three are set aside and resolved **after** all three flips are done (and
  can be redirected to other players as normal).

- **🛟 Second Chance** — Kept in front of you. The first time you would bust on a
  duplicate, **discard the duplicate and the Second Chance card** and keep playing. Notes:
  - You can hold **only one** Second Chance at a time. If you draw/are given a second one,
    you **must give it to another active player who doesn't already have one**. If no
    eligible player exists, it's **discarded**.
  - It only saves you from a **duplicate-number bust** — it can't stop a Freeze.

---

## Scoring (strict order)

For each player who **stayed or was frozen** (busted players score 0):

1. **Sum your number cards.**
2. **Apply ×2** if you have the multiplier (number total only).
3. **Add the +X modifiers** (+2 / +4 / +6 / +8 / +10).
4. **Add +15** if you achieved Flip 7.

*Example:* number cards 3 + 7 + 10 = 20 → ×2 = 40 → +4 modifier = **44**.

---

## Winning

At the **end of any round** in which at least one player has reached **200 points or
more**, the player with the **highest total wins**. If there's a tie at/above 200, play
additional rounds until one player is clearly ahead.

## Related edition

There's also a standalone sequel, **Flip 7: With a Vengeance** — negative/÷2 modifiers
played on opponents, Steal/Swap/Discard/Flip Four/Just One More, the Zero/Lucky 13/
Unlucky 7 specials, and no Second Chance ("no one's safe"). Its full card list and
rules are in [`flip7_vengeance_rules.md`](flip7_vengeance_rules.md). This document
covers the **original Flip 7** only.
